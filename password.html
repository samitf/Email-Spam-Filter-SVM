How to create an app password ?

An app password is a special code that allows a less secure app or device to access your email account. You may need an app password if you use a third-party email app that does not support two-step verification.

To create an app password for each service, follow these steps:

Gmail

  1. Go to your Google Account.
  2. Select Security.
  3. Under "Signing in to Google," select App Passwords. You may need to sign in and enable 2-Step Verification.
  4. At the bottom, choose Select app and choose the app you are using. Then choose Select device and choose the device you are using.
  5. Select Generate. A 16-digit password will appear.
  6. Copy and paste this password into your email app.

Outlook

  1. Go to your Microsoft Account.
  2. Select Security info.
  3. Select Add method, choose App password from the list, and then select Add.
  4. Enter a name for the app password, and then select Next. A password will appear.
  5. Copy and paste this password into your email app.

Yahoo

  1. Go to your Yahoo Mail.
  2. Select Account Info.
  3. Select Account Security. You may need to sign in and enable 2-Step Verification.
  4. Select Generate app password or Manage app passwords.
  5. Select your email app from the drop-down list or type it in if it's not listed.
  6. Select Generate password. A password will appear.
  7. Copy and paste this password into your email app.
